How to build to iOS 6 beta with Xcode 4.3

4 years ago July 6th, 2012

Apple doesn’t allow building to iOS 6 beta devices from Xcode 4.3, since it doesn’t include  the iOS 6 SDK. However, you may need to use Xcode 4.3 to publish apps to App Store, and doesn’t want to install 2 Xcode apps, or you just don’t want to use the buggy 4.5 betas.

Here is a way to build to iOS 6 beta devices with Xcode 4.3:

  1. Download the Xcode 4.5 beta dmg from Apple, and the mount the dmg
  2. Either copy the Xcode45-DP2.app to your hard disk (rest assumes you copied to /Applications) or just copy from the mounted dmg
  3. You need to copy /Applications/Xcode45-DP2.app/Contents/Developer/Platforms/iPhoneOS.platform/DeviceSupport/6.0 to /Applications/Xcode.app/Contents/Developer/Platforms/iPhoneOS.platform/DeviceSupport
  4. Also, copy /Applications/Xcode45-DP2.app/Contents/Developer/Platforms/iPhoneOS.platform/Developer/SDKs/iPhoneOS6.0.sdk to  /Applications/Xcode.app/Contents/Developer/Platforms/iPhoneOS.platform/Developer/SDKs/
  5. Finally, copy the version.plist in the iPhoneOS.platform

Now you should be able to build to your iOS 6 device from Xcode 4.3

Uncrushing images with pngcrush

4 years ago July 3rd, 2012

Apple optimizes PNG files in iOS apps with the pngcrush utility, and these can only be viewed by iOS by default. For Mac OS X to correctly read these files, they have to be uncrushed.

Here is how you can do it with Xcode 4.3.x, and files will be in the reverted subdirectory.

/Applications/Xcode.app/Contents/Developer/Platforms/iPhoneOS.platform/Developer/usr/bin/pngcrush -dir reverted -revert-iphone-optimizations -q *.png

This also works for the Retina iPad pngs.

Update: iOS7 introduced asset catalogs, where you can’t extract the assets this way since they are compiled into a “assets.car” file. However, you can use this tool to extract them.
